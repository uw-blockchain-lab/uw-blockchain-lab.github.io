Soubhik Deb

Hi! I am Soubhik (pronounced as Shaw-bik). Currently, I am a second year Ph.D. student at the University of Washington Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering, advised by Prof. Sreeram Kannan in the Information Theory Lab. I am a big believer in democratization of trust, data and control in digital platforms and permissionless systems like blockchain are expected to play a critical role in achieving this. However, applications of Blockchain outside of cryptocurrencies is still limited in scale and concept. So, over the course of two years in my Ph.D. journey, I have undertaken a first-principles, full-stack approach for pursuing research on blockchains: viewing blockchains as an integrated system involving interplay among networking, distributed consensus, data storage, incentives, and applications. This complex interplay can be distilled down to four layers:peer-to-peer (P2P), consensus, scaling and application. The projects that I have contributed to has enhanced the performance of blockchains in different layers and pioneered novel paradigms for permissionless systems.

Previously…

I obtained my PhD from Carnegie Mellon University in May 2019, where I was advised by Noah Smith and Chris Dyer. During most of my PhD I was a visiting student at the University of Washington in Seattle.
